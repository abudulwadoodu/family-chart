# Welcome to Your Family Tree 🌳

This guide walks you through the everyday things you'll do most often: browsing your family's story on the Timeline, editing details, sharing memories, and reacting to the moments that matter. No technical know-how needed — just a browser and a bit of curiosity about your family history.

---

## 1. Navigating the Timeline

The **Timeline** is where your tree's events live — births, weddings, graduations, reunions, anything you or another family member has added.

- Open the **Timeline** page from your tree's navigation. You'll see every event for the tree, automatically **grouped by year**, with the most recent years at the top and undated events tucked into their own "Undated" group.
- Use the two filter chips at the top — **All events** and **My events** — to switch between everything in the tree and just the events you personally added.
- Click any event in the list to open its **detail view**, where you'll find the full date, location, description, tagged participants, attached photos, and the comment thread for that event.
- Use the breadcrumb at the top of the detail view to jump back to the Timeline list (or all the way out to your tree).

> 💡 **Tip:** The Timeline shows a simple year-by-year list rather than a zoomable chart — think of it like flipping through chapters of a family photo album, one year at a time.

---

## 2. Editing Events

Found a typo, or want to add more detail to an event? Every field edits **independently**, so you only ever touch the one piece of information you actually want to change.

Open an event from the Timeline list to reach its detail view, then look for the small **pencil icon (✏️)** next to each field:

- Click the pencil next to the **Title** to rename the event.
- Click the pencil next to the **Date** to change when it happened.
- Click the pencil next to the **Location** to update where it happened (or add one for the first time via the "Add a location…" prompt).
- Click the pencil next to the **Description** to add or edit the story behind the event (or "Add a description…" if there isn't one yet).

Each pencil opens a tiny inline form for **just that field** — no pop-up windows, no page reloads, and editing one field never disturbs the others. Click **Save** to confirm, or **Cancel** to back out without changes.

> 📌 **Note:** Sharing/visibility (who can see the event) has its own separate control — a small badge near the title — so you won't accidentally change who can see something while you're just fixing a typo.

**Editing photos, too:** The same per-field pencil pattern works on photos and documents — click the pencil next to a photo's title or description to edit just that piece inline.

---

## 3. Sharing Memories

Every event and every photo has its own **comment thread** — the perfect place for family members to add context, share a memory, or just say hello.

- Scroll to the **Comments** section on any event or photo's detail view.
- Type your update or memory into the box at the **top** of the comment thread and click **Post**.
- Your comment appears immediately, right below the box — the stream always shows the **newest comments first**, so there's no scrolling to the bottom to find what was just said.
- Posted a comment by mistake? You can delete your own comments any time using the trash icon next to them.

> 💡 **Tip:** Comments are a great way to add family stories that don't fit neatly into an event's Description — think of it as the conversation happening around the memory.

---

## 4. Interacting with Timeline Media

When you're viewing a photo, video, or document attached to an event or your tree's media library, you can react to it in one click:

- Look for the row of **emoji reaction chips** — 👍 ❤️ 😂 😮 😢 🙏 — near the top of the media view.
- Click any emoji to add your reaction. Click it again to remove it. You can only have one active reaction per item at a time, and switching to a different emoji swaps your reaction automatically.
- If others have reacted too, you'll see a small count next to each emoji showing how many people picked it.

Beyond reactions, you can also:

- **Tag family members** who appear in a photo — start typing a name and pick them from the suggestions.
- **Comment** directly on the photo, using the same comment thread described above.

---

That's it! The best way to get comfortable is to click around — nothing here is destructive, and you can always edit things again later. Happy exploring! 🌿
